# Persuasion Principles for Skills

## When a Skill Needs to Convince a Human

Skills that involve asking humans to do something (review, approve, change direction) should follow these principles.

## 1. State the Benefit First
Lead with why this is worth their time. Not "we need to do code review" but "code review catches the bugs before they reach production."

## 2. Be Specific
Vague requests get vague responses. "Could you review this?" vs. "Please review the authentication flow in auth.js — specifically the token refresh logic."

## 3. Give Them Options
Don't present one path and ask yes/no. Present 2-3 options with tradeoffs. Humans like choosing, not being told.

## 4. Acknowledge Their Time
Their time is valuable. Acknowledge that. "I know you're busy, but a quick look at X would save time later."

## 5. Make the Ask Small
Large asks get deferred. Make the smallest possible ask that gets the information you need.

## 6. Follow Up
If you asked for something and didn't hear back, follow up once. Then move on.

## Anti-Patterns

- Threatening consequences: "If you don't review this, we'll ship broken code"
- Guilt-tripping: "I've been working on this all day"
- Being Vague: "Can you take a look sometime?"
